About Gujrat

Gujarat is located on the western coast of India and has the longest coastline of 1,600 km among all the states in the country. It is bounded by Pakistan to the northwest and by the Indian states of Rajasthan to the north, Madhya Pradesh to the east and Maharashtra to the southeast. While Gandhinagar is the capital city, Ahmedabad is the largest city in the state and one of the most important textile centres in India

Some of the most popular tourist destinations in Gujrat include:

  • Ahmedabad is the largest city in the State of Gujarat and the Seventh- largest urban agglomeration in India. The city was also at the heart of India's struggle for independence from the British rule, with Mahatma Gandhi residing at Sabarmati Ashram here.


  • Kutch (or Kachchh), refers to the fact that it alternates between wet (submerged during the monsoon season) and dry. Much of Kutch consists of seasonal wetlands known as the Great Rann of Kutch (famous for its salt desert) and smaller Little Rann of Kutch (famous for its Wild Ass Sanctuary).


  • TheStatue Of Unity, is the world's tallest statue, with a height of 182 metres, located near Kevadia in the state of Gujarat, India. It depicts Indian statesman and independence activist Vallabhbhai Patel.


  • Gir Forest National Park is a wildlife sanctuary in Gujarat, western India. It was established to protect Asiatic lions, who frequent the fenced-off Devalia Safari Park, along with leopards and antelopes. Gir Jungle Trail, outside the fenced area, traverses deciduous forest and is home to wildlife including vultures and pythons.


  • Surat is one of the cleanest city of India and is also known by several other names like “THE SILK CITY”, “THE DIAMOND CITY”, “THE GREEN CITY”, etc. It has the most vibrant present and an equally varied heritage of the past. It is the city where the British first land in India.

    Somnath Temple

    Somnath is a magnificent temple situated in Sagar Kant of Saurashtra in Gujarat state. One of the 12 holy Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva is in Jyotirlinga here in Somnath. Somnath is also mentioned in Rigveda. It believed to be the place where the first of the twelve holy jyotirlingas emerged in India – a spot where Shiva appeared as a fiery column of light. The Jyotirlinga is adorned with flowers, ornaments of silver and gold and people from far and wide come to worship it.

  • Nearby places to visit: Somnath Beach, Pandav Gufa, Baan Stambh
  • Best time to Visit: Anytime

    • Dwarka

    Dwarka is believed to have been the first capital of Gujarat. The city's name means the “gateway to heaven” in Sanskrit, as Dwar means “gate” and ka means “Brahma”. Dwarka has also been referred to throughout its history as “Mokshapuri”, “Dwarkamati”, and “Dwarkavati”. It is well known for its temples and a pilgrimage centre for Hindus. The Dwarakadhisa Temple, also called Jagat Mandir, located in the heart of Dwarka, is a Vaishnava temple. It was built by Raja Jagat Singh Rathore, so it is called Jagat Mandir.

  • Nearby places to visit: Dwarkadhish Temple, Sudama Setu, Dwarka Beach
  • Best time to Visit: Anytime

    • Sabarmati Ashram

    Sabarmati Ashram is situated on the banks of the Sabarmati river in Ahmedabad. Gandhiji and his wife Kasturba lived here from 1917 and 1930. Gandhiji led the Dandi march or the the Salt Satyagraha from the Ashram on March 12, 1930. To serve as an institution that would carry on a search for truth and a platform to bring together a group of workers committed to non-violence who would help secure freedom for India. The Ashram was established as a national monument after the March .

  • Nearby places to visit: Sabarmati Riverfront SVP National Museum
  • Best time to Visit: Anytime
